# Drive Table Tennis — v2.0 Design Uplift Plan

**Author:** design pass via bencium-impact-designer
**Date:** 2026-06-02
**Status:** Strategy / direction — not yet a build spec

---

## 0. Where we are (honest audit)

**The concept is strong and ownable: office ping-pong as a motorsport championship.** It's brand-true (Drive = cars) and already executed with real craft:

- **Type:** IBM Plex Sans / Mono / Condensed — characterful, brand-mandated, not generic. Keep it.
- **Colour:** Drive blue `#2960c5`, coral `#ff5e55`, warm off-white, near-black ink. Tasteful light theme.
- **Signature components:** a real 270° **tachometer** for ELO, a looping **pixel rally** hero, the **"Starting Grid"** leaderboard with *pole*, *gap-to-leader*, and framer-motion **row reordering**, an **In-Form** card with count-up deltas.
- **Motion + a11y:** framer-motion layout animation, count-up counters, `prefers-reduced-motion` respected.

**So this is a "commit harder + add the missing loop" job, not a rescue.**

### What's missing (the gap between "nice dashboard" and "thing people open daily")
1. **No personal identity.** The public app doesn't know *who you are*, so nothing is "yours" — no your-rank, your-streak, your-rivals. This is the single biggest retention gap.
2. **No time-bound stakes.** Everything is all-time. No seasons, no "this week", no urgency, no fresh-start moments.
3. **No streaks / momentum / achievements** despite the full match history being available to compute them.
4. **No rivalries / head-to-head**, despite having every result needed for "you vs Marcus 7–3".
5. **No social loop.** Recent matches is a static list, not a feed. No reactions, no banter, no nudges back into the app.
6. **Logging a game has no payoff moment.** It saves and resets — no reveal, no drama, no dopamine.
7. **Light-only.** A dark "Night Race" mode would add drama, choice, and daily-driver comfort.

## 1. The three questions, answered

### What would I change?
- **Give the home a "you" anchor.** A lightweight, no-auth "I am ___" pick (stored locally) that personalises the dashboard: your rating on the tacho, your grid position, your streak, your next target, your rivals' latest results. Everything else stays public.
- **Reframe the leaderboard as a live Championship**, not an all-time table — add a season selector and "this season" standings on top of all-time.
- **Turn "Recent" into an Activity Feed** — social, reactable, with ELO deltas and one-tap "View game".
- **Add a dark "Night Race" theme** (palette below) with a toggle; make the racing concept sing in the dark.
- **Make logging pay off** — a "lights-out" start + tachometer-sweep result reveal.
- **Tighten hierarchy** — one hero number per screen (your rating / the champion / the result), bigger condensed numerals (motorsport timing-screen energy).

### What would make it more fun & engaging?
- **A race-result moment**: log a game → 5-red-lights → green → the two tachometer needles sweep from old→new ELO while deltas count up → confetti if someone takes a position. ~1.2s of theatre.
- **Status & titles**: *Pole Sitter*, *Giant Killer* (beat someone +200), *Backmarker*, *On a Charge* (3+ win streak), *Nemesis* badges — collectible, on-brand.
- **Streaks with loss-aversion**: a visible 🔥 streak people play to protect.
- **Head-to-head "telemetry" pages**: vs-record, who-leads, last-5, biggest upset.
- **Personality in microcopy**: "P4 — two wins off the podium", "You haven't raced in 6 days".

### What would increase usage / bring people back?
Ranked by retention impact:
1. **Slack integration** (Drive lives in Slack). Auto-post race-result cards to a channel; nudge DMs like "🏁 Marcus took P3 from you" and a weekly standings post. *This is the #1 office-ladder retention lever* — it pulls people back through the channel they already live in.
2. **Seasons / "Grands Prix"** — monthly seasons, a champion crowned and archived, soft ELO decay/regression toward the mean at reset. Fresh starts create recurring re-engagement spikes.
3. **Personal identity + "next target"** — a concrete reason to play *today* ("+22 to overtake Priya").
4. **Streaks & achievements** — collect, protect, show off.
5. **Shareable cards** — a "season wrapped" / match-result card image to drop in Slack = in-org virality.

## 2. v2.0 creative direction (commit boldly)

### Recommended — Direction A: **"Race Control"** (deepen the championship)
Take the existing motorsport metaphor all the way:
- **Seasons = Grands Prix.** Standings, a crowned champion, archives.
- **Dark "Night Race" mode** as a first-class theme (toggle), light = "Day Race".
- **Telemetry** = stats/head-to-head. **The Paddock** = players. **Race Control** = the live feed.
- **Race-result theatre** on every logged game (lights-out + tacho sweep).
- **Trophies** = achievements. **Pit wall** Slack bot = nudges + result cards.

Why: it's the natural, ownable evolution, 100% brand-true, and the data already supports it. Nothing here feels bolted on.

### Alternative — Direction B: **"Broadcast"** (sports-TV graphics)
Live-broadcast aesthetic: lower-thirds, tickers, a big scoreboard, motion-graphic stat reveals, "LIVE NOW at the table". More kinetic and energetic; great for a wall-mounted office screen. Trade-off: heavier motion, less brand-specific, more visual noise to manage.

### Alternative — Direction C: **"Arcade"** (retro pixel game)
Lean into the pixel rally → full 8-bit/arcade: high-score tables, pixel trophies, chiptune SFX, CRT glow. Maximum fun and personality. Trade-off: reads less premium/credible for a Drive-branded product; risks novelty wearing off.

**Recommendation: A, with a dash of B's live-feed and C's celebratory SFX.**

## 3. Design system v2.0 (foundations)

### 3.1 "Night Race" dark theme (new)
A blue-black motorsport night palette (validated in earlier mockups):
```
--background: #0e1116   (blue-black)
--card:       #11151c
--border:     #232a35
--foreground: #e6e9ef
--muted-fg:   #7d8694
--primary:    #2960c5   (Drive blue)   / glow accent: #3b97fa
--accent:     #ff5e55   (coral, pops on dark)
--gain:       #3fb950   (or keep blue for ELO-up; coral for down)
--loss:       #f85149
```
- Theme toggle in the header (sun/moon → "Day Race / Night Race"). Persist in `localStorage`, respect `prefers-color-scheme` on first visit.
- Implement as a `.dark` class on `<html>` driving the existing CSS variables (tokens already centralised in `globals.css` — low effort).

### 3.2 Type
- Keep IBM Plex (brand). **Push IBM Plex Condensed harder** for hero numbers — huge tabular condensed numerals = trackside timing-screen. Add a display scale step (`clamp()`-fluid) for "hero stat" moments.

### 3.3 Motion tokens
- Easings: `--ease-rev` (ease-out for needle sweeps), `--ease-snap` (overshoot for position changes).
- Durations: micro 120ms, reveal 320ms, theatre 1.2s.
- A single orchestrated home-load stagger (60–80ms) — leaderboard rows cascade in.
- Grain/vignette overlay on dark mode for atmosphere (subtle, `pointer-events:none`).

### 3.4 New shared components
- `StatHero` — the one big number per screen (your rating / champion / result).
- `RaceResult` — the lights-out + dual-tacho-sweep reveal (used by logger success + match modal).
- `StreakPill`, `FormDots` (last-5 W/L), `NextTarget`, `HeadToHead`, `TrophyChip`.
- `ActivityFeed` — the social restyle of recent matches.
- `SeasonBadge` / `ChampionCard`.

## 5. Phased plan (prioritised by impact ÷ effort, simplicity-first)

> Constraint honoured: **simplicity is primary; avoid clever infra.** Phases 1–2 are almost entirely front-end + compute-from-existing-data. Heavier infra is isolated to Phase 3 and clearly flagged.

### Phase 1 — Foundations & delight (front-end only, no backend)
- Night Race dark theme + toggle (token-driven; low risk).
- Motion tokens + orchestrated home-load stagger.
- `RaceResult` theatre on logging + in the view-game modal (tacho sweep + lights + count-up; confetti on position change).
- `StatHero`, `StreakPill`, `FormDots` components (streak/form computed from existing match history — pure functions, unit-testable like `lib/match-format.ts`).
- Leaderboard → podium + pole shimmer + rank-change arrows.
**Outcome:** dramatically more fun, zero new infra, zero schema change.

### Phase 2 — Identity, rivalry & feed (light backend / mostly compute)
- "I am ___" local identity → personalised home (no auth, `localStorage`).
- `HeadToHead` rivalry pages + `NextTarget` (computed from history).
- Trophies/achievements (computed from history; a small `lib/achievements.ts` of pure rules).
- `ActivityFeed` restyle of Race Log; optional lightweight reactions (small `reactions` table — the only schema add).
**Outcome:** the app becomes personal and social; reasons to return daily.

### Phase 3 — Retention engines (bigger bets — flagged for cost)
- **Slack integration** (highest retention ROI): race-result cards + nudge DMs + weekly standings. Needs a webhook/bot + a posting job. *Medium infra — recommend as the top "bigger bet".*
- **Seasons / Grands Prix**: season model, scheduled close, champion archive, soft ELO decay at reset. Medium infra (a `seasons` table + a close routine).
- **Shareable "Season Wrapped" cards**: server-rendered image cards for Slack/social. Medium infra (image generation).
**Outcome:** recurring re-engagement and in-org virality. Each item is independently shippable; do them only if the loop in Phases 1–2 proves out.

## 6. Guardrails
- **Brand:** stay within Drive's IBM Plex + blue/coral; the racing metaphor is the differentiator — don't dilute it.
- **Simplicity:** prefer computing from existing match data over new tables; isolate any infra to Phase 3; keep components small and focused (the `lib/*.ts` pure-function + thin-component pattern already in the repo).
- **Accessibility:** maintain WCAG AA contrast in *both* themes; all theatre/motion must no-op under `prefers-reduced-motion`; keep keyboard/focus states.
- **Verification:** per project norm, validate data-driven UI with throwaway preview fixtures, never by seeding live Supabase.

---

## 7. Suggested first move
Ship **Phase 1** behind no flag (it's all front-end and immediately raises the "fun" ceiling), starting with the **Night Race theme** and the **RaceResult reveal** — the two highest-delight, lowest-risk wins. Then decide on Direction A vs B/C before investing in Phase 2+.

## 8. Visual audit notes (from live screenshots, dev server, 2026-06-02)

Captured the running app (home, expanded logger, player profile, Race Log, view-game modal, mobile). Confirms the recent work and sharpens the critique:

**Confirmed working / good:**
- Centered logger no longer spills — expands cleanly with steppers, stopwatch, date.
- Universal "View game" button reads consistently across Race Log, Recent, and player history.
- View-game modal is clean and correct (set-by-set with winner bolded, "Sean wins 3-1", ELO +16/-16 with before-after, duration) — the ideal `RaceResult` target.
- Tachometer ELO renders beautifully on the profile; mobile single-column is naturally responsive. No console errors.

**Sharpened issues (screenshot-grounded):**
1. **Sparse desktop canvas.** The `max-w-2xl` centered column leaves large empty margins on desktop; with little data the home/Race Log/profile feel lonely and static. v2.0 should *use the width* on large screens — e.g. a personalised "you" rail + standings, or a two-zone dashboard — while staying single-column on mobile.
2. **Empty/low-data states are flat.** "Nobody has played 3+ matches...", a one-row Race Log, a flat single-point ELO trajectory. These need personality and a "get the grid going" nudge — first impressions for a new office are mostly empty states.
3. **Light + airy = low drama.** Reinforces the **Night Race** dark mode as a high-impact, low-risk win.
4. **The view-game modal is begging for motion** — static now; the needle-sweep + lights reveal would make every result feel like an event.
5. **Mobile header is cramped** at 375px (nav + "Log a game" button wraps). Add a compact/icon nav or condense on small screens.
6. **Minor copy:** the logger submit says "Save match" even in Quick mode — "Save game" fits Quick.

These don't change the plan's priorities — they reinforce Phase 1 (Night Race + RaceResult) and add two small front-end wins: a **wide-screen home layout** and **characterful empty states**.

## 9. v2 plan-set (LOCKED — all 5 pillars committed)

Shaped by the product Q&A: single ~15-person Drive office, ~10–20 games/wk, friendly-competitive banter, self-logged on web. North star: **a polished, credible, fun product people want to log into** → lifts logging rate + joins. Leaks to kill: *play-but-don't-log* and *never-join*. Explicit wants: weekly Slack digest + richer home stat viz.

All five pillars are in scope, delivered as four independent plans that each ship on their own and reuse the Plan 1 stats engine:

| Plan | File | Pillars | Infra |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1 — Stats Engine + Championship HQ Home** | `plans/2026-06-02-v2-plan1-stats-engine-and-home.md` | 1; foundation for 4 | none |
| **2 — Logging payoff (RaceResult) + Join glow-up** | `plans/…-v2-plan2-raceresult-and-join.md` | 2, 5 | none |
| **3 — Banter layer (titles + head-to-head matrix + badges)** | `plans/…-v2-plan3-banter.md` | 4 | none |
| **4 — Weekly Slack digest** | `plans/…-v2-plan4-slack-digest.md` | 3 | Slack webhook + Vercel cron |

Build order: **1 → 2 → 3 → 4** (4 last, since it depends on the webhook + reuses titles from 3). Night Race dark mode stays an optional polish item, slotted into whichever plan has room. Per workflow: no git in any plan (Mahir commits); subagents default to sonnet.
